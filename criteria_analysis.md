### Student Questionnaire Characteristics

Here are 21 key characteristics to consider for your scholarship application questionnaire:

#### I. Hardships & Personal Circumstances

1.  **Acute Financial Need:** Many scholarships prioritize students demonstrating significant financial hardship, often specified as "low-income," "economically disadvantaged," or "Pell Grant eligible."
2.  **Significant Health or Learning Challenges:** This includes a broad range of conditions that may require additional support or indicate resilience.
    *   **Learning Disabilities/Neurodiversity:** Explicitly mentions "Dyslexia," "ADHD," "Processing Disorders," "Autism," and general "Learning Differences."
    *   **Mental Health Condition:** Students who have personally struggled with mental health or been impacted by a family member's struggles.
    *   **Chronic Disease/Serious Physical Health Challenge:** Addressing conditions like "diabetes" or other long-term health issues.
3.  **Experience with Foster Care System:** A specific and important niche mentioned for several scholarships.
4.  **Loss of Parent/Guardian in Line of Duty:** For those who have lost a parent, guardian, or spouse serving as a "first responder (law-enforcement, fire, or military)."
5.  **Overcoming Significant Adversity:** A broader category that encompasses enduring profound losses or demonstrating tenacity in overcoming obstacles.

#### II. Demographics & Identity

6.  **First-Generation College Student:** A very frequently cited characteristic for students who are the first in their family to attend college.
7.  **Underrepresented Minority / BIPOC Identity:** Encompassing various ethnic and racial backgrounds often prioritized for diversity initiatives. Includes "BIPOC," "Hispanic/Latinx," "African American," "Native American," "Haitian American," "Samoan ethnicity," and "Asian American."
8.  **LGBTQIA+ Identity:** Explicitly mentioned for scholarships supporting LGBTQ+ students, often with a focus on leadership and community involvement.
9.  **Gender Identity (e.g., Female, Non-binary):** "Female" is a very strong and recurring characteristic, often for STEM, Aviation, or leadership roles. "Non-binary" is also mentioned.
10. **Veteran / Military Affiliation (Self or Family):** Includes "Veterans," "Military Service" members, or "children of wounded warriors/active-duty members/veterans."
11. **Mensa Member:** A highly specific, niche characteristic, but explicitly mentioned as eligibility for some awards.

#### III. Academic Interests & Career Paths

12. **Aviation (Pilot, Maintenance, Management):** This is by far the most prominent and detailed category in the data.
    *   **Pilot Training:** Covering all levels from "Private Pilot Certificate" to "Commercial," "Instrument Rating," "CFI (Flight Instructor)," and "ATP (Airline Transport Pilot)" certificates/ratings, including "Rotorcraft/Helicopter" and "Advanced Ratings" like "tailwheel" or "warbird."
    *   **Aviation Maintenance:** "A&P mechanic," "Aircraft Mechanic Training," "Avionics Technician."
    *   **Aviation Management/Operations:** "Flight Dispatcher," "Airport Manager," "Air Traffic Controller."
13. **STEM Fields (Science, Technology, Engineering, Math):** A broad and consistently high-demand area. Includes "Aerospace/Aeronautical Engineering," "Electrical Engineering," "Mechanical Engineering," "Computer Science," "Biosciences," "Chemistry," "Marine Science," "Technology industry," and general "STEM Education."
14. **Healthcare Fields:** Another major area of focus. Includes "Nursing" (especially "Long Term Care"), "Medicine," "Pre-Med," "Nurse Anesthesia," and "Mental Health" as a career.
15. **Skilled Trades & Construction:** A significant category encompassing vocational training. Includes general "Trades," "Construction Management," "Building," "Construction Technology," "HVAC," "Plumbing," "Welding," and "Automotive industry careers."
16. **Business & Finance (including Entrepreneurship):** Covers "Financial industry," "Banking," "Accounting," "Marketing," "Finance," and "Entrepreneurship" and "Innovation."
17. **Public Service & Social Impact:** For students interested in careers that serve the community. Includes "Social Work," "First Responder (firefighter, police, EMT)," "Community Improvement," and "progressives movements for social justice."
18. **Law / Legal Studies:** Mentioned for students pursuing "Legal Education / Pre-Law" or "Paralegal" careers.
19. **Arts & Humanities (e.g., Music Education, Graphic Design):** Includes "Instrumental (Band) Music Education," "Arts Education," and "Graphic Communication/Printing Technology."

#### IV. Skills & Experiences

20. **Leadership & Community Service/Volunteering:** A very common requirement, demonstrating engagement beyond academics.
21. **Athletic Participation (e.g., Golf, Refereeing):** Specific mentions of "Golf," general "Athletics," and "Sportsmanship/Refereeing."
